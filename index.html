Daim is a popular chocolate candy known for its crunchy almond caramel center coated in smooth milk chocolate.
Daim was created by Marabou in Sweden in the 1950s.
It was launched under the name Dajm throughout Sweden and Norway the next year,
Finland in 1963, and Denmark in 1971.
It was renamed "Daim" in most countries in 1990,
although it continued to be marketed in the United Kingdom and Ireland as Dime.
